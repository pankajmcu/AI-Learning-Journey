    Prompting

    Prompting is the practice of instructing a large language model (LLM) to perform a task.
    The structure and clarity of the prompt strongly influence the quality of the response.

    Core Principles

    1. Role — define the model’s persona

      “You are a helpful Python tutor.”

    2. Goal — specify the exact task

      “Explain decorators in simple terms.”

    3. Constraints — add rules

      “Use bullet points, max 5 lines.”

    4. Format — define output structure

      “Return JSON with keys: title, summary.”

    5. Examples (few-shot) — show inputs & outputs 

      English: Hello → French: Bonjour  
English: Thank you → French: Merci  
English: Good night → French:

    Prompting Techniques

    • Zero-shot → Direct instruction without examples
      Prompt: 

      Translate "machine learning" into Spanish.
      Output: 
      aprendizaje automático

    • Few-shot → Include examples to guide behavior
      Prompt: 

      English: Hello → French: Bonjour
English: Thank you → French: Merci
English: Good night → French:
      Output: 
      Bonne nuit

    • Chain of Thought (CoT) → Ask for step-by-step reasoning
      Prompt: 
      Solve step by step:
A pen costs $1 and a notebook costs $2. 
If I buy 3 pens and 2 notebooks, how much do I spend in total?
      Output: 
      Step 1: Cost of 3 pens = 3 × $1 = $3
Step 2: Cost of 2 notebooks = 2 × $2 = $4
Step 3: Total = $3 + $4 = $7
Final Answer: $7
    • ReAct → Reasoning combined with external actions/tools Prompt: 
      Task: Find out who won the 2018 FIFA World Cup and explain in one sentence.
Plan: 
1. Search for the answer.
2. Return a short explanation.
      Output: 
      The 2018 FIFA World Cup was won by France, who defeated Croatia 4–2 in the final.
    • Self-critique → generate, evaluate, and refine outputs
      Prompt: 
      Write a two-sentence summary of what transformers are. 
Then critique the summary. 
Finally, provide an improved version.
      Output: 
      Initial Summary:
Transformers are deep learning models that use attention to process sequences. 
They are widely used for NLP tasks.

Critique:
The explanation is too brief and lacks context about their significance.

Improved Summary:
Transformers are neural network architectures built around self-attention, 
allowing them to model long-range dependencies in text efficiently. 
They form the foundation of modern large language models used in NLP and beyond.

    Anti-Patterns

    • Vague requests (“Explain AI.”)
    • Long prompts with key info buried at the end
    • Implicit requirements not stated clearly
    • Missing or inconsistent output format

    Why Prompting Matters

    • Saves tokens → lower cost & faster responses
    • Produces predictable outputs
    • Enables automation and evaluation when outputs are structured
